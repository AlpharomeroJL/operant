Scheduling Workflows

How the scheduler engine works, and what running on a schedule will look like once it is wired to the app.

What Is Wired Today

Running a saved workflow again with one click is available now. Starting a schedule from the app is not wired up yet: the scheduler engine described below (cron times, file watches, window and email triggers) is built and tested, but the app cannot yet create a schedule for you. This guide describes what the engine already does and what setting a schedule will look like once it is connected. Nothing on this page runs on its own in this release.

When Workflows Will Run By Themselves

Once scheduling is connected to the app, a saved workflow will run automatically with no click from you. These are the trigger types the engine already supports:

Time-Based Scheduling (Every Day, Week, or Month)

You can set a workflow to run at a specific time:

  • Every weekday at 9 AM
  • Every Monday
  • The first day of every month at 5 PM
  • Every two hours

When you click "Schedule", Operant will show you plain language options. Pick the one that matches your task. Operant handles all the complicated schedule stuff behind the scenes.

File Watching

If your workflow processes files (like invoices or receipts), you can tell Operant to run it automatically whenever a new file shows up in a specific folder. For example, "Run this workflow every time a PDF arrives in my Downloads folder".

Email Triggers

Workflows can run when a new email arrives with certain words in the subject line. For example, "Run my invoice workflow every time I get an email from accounting@vendor.com with 'Invoice' in the subject".

Window Appears

You can run a workflow whenever you open a specific app. For example, "Run this workflow every time I open Excel".

Manual On Demand

You can also just click a button whenever you feel like it and Operant will run the workflow immediately.

Safety: Operant Asks Before Running

The first time you set up a schedule, Operant will ask you to approve it. You will see a clear list of what the workflow will do and which apps or files it needs to access. You approve it, and then it runs on schedule automatically.

See What Happened

After each run, you can see what Operant did: when it ran, if it succeeded, how long it took, and what changed. Click the Operant tray icon and look for "Recent Runs" or "Activity". If something went wrong, you will see what the problem was and how to fix it.

Stop or Edit a Scheduled Workflow

Change your mind? Click on any scheduled workflow and select "Edit Schedule" to change when it runs, or "Turn Off" to stop it from running automatically. You can turn it back on anytime.

# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

We are committed to providing a welcoming and inspiring community for all people, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge that those participating in our community, whether online or at in-person events, will experience a harassment-free environment free of discrimination based on any of the above characteristics.

## Our values

Our community is built on:
- Curiosity and learning: we ask questions, share knowledge, and help each other grow
- Respect and inclusion: we listen to and value the perspective of every contributor
- Transparency: we discuss decisions openly and explain our choices
- Safety: we create space where people feel secure to speak up and be themselves

## Our standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:
- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback
- Focusing on what is best for the community
- Showing empathy towards other community members
- Crediting the work and ideas of others

Examples of unacceptable behavior:
- Harassing, insulting, or derogatory comments or personal attacks
- Trolling, intentionally stirring up conflict, or arguing in bad faith
- Publishing others' private information without consent
- Unwelcome sexual attention or advances
- Intimidation, threats, or incitement of violence
- Spam or low-effort, off-topic posts
- Advocating for or protecting those who engage in the above behavior

## Scope

This code of conduct applies to all community spaces, including:
- GitHub issues, pull requests, and Discussions
- Discord, Slack, or other chat servers associated with the project
- In-person events and conferences
- Any other forums where the community gathers in the project's name

## Enforcement

Community leaders are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior. They will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to violations.

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported by contacting the project maintainer via email or in a private message. All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.

Project maintainers who violate this code of conduct will be held to the same standard as others and may be removed from leadership positions.
